eBay Investigation Procedures

You may have wondered what steps eBay takes when investigating allegations of misuse on eBay. This outline explains the steps included in researching claims of misuse, and the review of your e-mail reports. As you will see, even with these procedures, the process of protecting against fraud and misuse takes time.


A Note from the eBay SafeHarbor Team
Please be patient! Investigations can take several days. Sending more e-mail messages will result in additional delay. When reporting cases, please include item numbers and email addresses, not email attachments that may contain feedback profiles or item descriptions.

Please note: Safe Harbor does not investigate non-delivery of items, bid non-performance or feedback issues (except shilling), among others. For a list of what should not be reported to Safe Harbor, along with helpful suggestions of where to turn in such cases, please click here.

For the latest system updates, please check the Announcement Board.

"Please remember. In order to leave a complaint, you must be a registered eBay user. If your e-mail address does not appear in our registry, we must respectfully decline to investigate or take action. The resources and facilities of our Investigation Team are reserved for the use of our registered users. Please, only report suspicious activities with your registered address."


This document comprises several pages, all of which will help guide you through an investigation.

  • First of all, we will discuss what is not a misuse of the system. These items are, for the most part, not part of our charter and are not investigated. We offer a listing of these actions, and what you may do about them, here.
  • A discussion of the types of misuse eBay can investigate may be found here. This is a listing of the types of abuse and misuse of the eBay system that we can investigate and take action against, when warranted. Also included is a listing of what types of information we are looking for when performing these investigations. This will help you supply us with the information we need to investigate alleged fraud or misuse.
  • You can read about our Investigation Procedures on this page. This page describes the actual steps that we follow when investigating misuse.
