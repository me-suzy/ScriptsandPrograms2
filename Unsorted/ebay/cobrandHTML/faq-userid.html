    • What is a User ID?
    A User ID is a "nickname" you can use to participate on eBay.

    UNDERSTANDING WHY...

    Why does eBay use User IDs?
    eBay believes in an open and honest marketplace. But, as many of you are painfully aware, "robots" are sent to eBay from time to time to collect the e-mail addresses of our users. These e-mail addresses later received unsolicited e-mail or "spam". Robot attacks not only expose users to spam, but they can also seriously degrade system performance. User IDs will make it more difficult for these robots to collect your e-mail address while protecting your communication with your eBay friends.

    Why did eBay do this?
    eBay wanted to help you protect your e-mail address from these robots and the spam sent by companies using the robots. User IDs will make it more difficult for these robots to collect your e-mail address while protecting your communication with your eBay friends.

    Why did it take so long for eBay to do this? 
    eBay was launched in September 1995. For two years, we operated within an open and honest marketplace, where users werent forced to hide their e-mail addresses. While we did not want the actions of a few companies to force us into unnecessarily hiding e-mail addresses, we did recognize that our community members deserved protection and privacy.

    eBay has worked diligently these last few months to find a solution which protects your e-mail address from robots and spam and which still upholds the values of the open and honest marketplace you have created here at eBay. We believe that User IDs will do just that.

    Why should I have a User ID?
    Your default User ID will be your registered e-mail address. But you might want to consider using a User ID if you are concerned about spam.

    WHO AM I...

    Can I choose my own User ID?
    Yes! Your family may have named you when you were born. Your friends may have called you names when you were in school. But this is your chance to nickname yourself anything you want.

    Do I have to choose my own User ID?
    No. If you dont want to choose your User ID, eBay will use your registered e-mail address as your User ID.

    Why wont eBay just invent User IDs for everyone like other sites do?
    Before User IDs, people were already nicknaming themselves and referring to each other by nickname. The introduction of User IDs just formalized that which we already knew: There is a real person behind an e-mail address.

    What can my User ID be?
    Your User ID may be a number of things. Here are some guidelines.

    A User ID may contain letters (a-z), numbers (0-9) and/or symbols.

          These symbols, among others, are not permitted:

    • You may not use the "@" symbol unless your User ID is the same as your registered e-mail address. This is to prevent anyone else from using your e-mail address as his/her User ID.
    • You may not use the "&" symbol. Skippy said so.

    A User ID must be at least two (2) characters long.

    A User ID is case insensitive.

    A User ID may not contain spaces.

    A User ID may not be obscene, profane or hateful.


    Think about it. Using a User ID doesnt mean that other eBay users dont know who you are!

    We suggest that you apply the "Mom or Dad" Rule of Decency when choosing a User ID.
    What would Mom or Dad say if they knew you were using obscenity, profanity or hateful language in a public place? Better yet. What would they do to you?

    If you think Mom or Dad wouldnt approve, you can probably be sure that your fellow eBay users wont be too impressed either.  Why do you care? If you are a seller and a potential buyer sees your User ID and it doesnt say, "Im a trustworthy, honest person," he/she is less likely to bid on your item. No trust, no bids. If you are a bidder and a seller sees your User ID and it doesnt say, "Im a trustworthy, honest person," he/she is less likely to take your bid seriously. No trust, no sale.

    Think about it.

    What should my User ID be?
    Your default User ID will be your registered e-mail address. But you may also use your name or a creative nickname as your User ID. Here are a few suggestions if youre stuck.

    • Refer to your favorite collecting area.

      • If your name is Roger and you collect Rabbit figurines, you might request a User ID such as "Roger-Rabbit."
    • Refer to your business or occupation.

      • If you use eBay to sell pet supplies business, you might request a User ID such as "ACME_Roadrunner_Kits or Roadrunner_Hunter."
    • Refer to your hometown or state.

      • If your name is Casper and you happen to live in Wyoming, you might request a User ID such as "Casper_Wyoming."
    • Refer to your own sense of style.

      • If you consider yourself a person of tremendous strength, you might request a User ID such as "Man-of-Steel."
       
    Will anyone else have the same User ID?
    No. A User ID must be unique. In the real world, there can be thousands of people named "Robert." At eBay, only one person can have "Robert" as a User ID.

    Can my User ID be my e-mail address?
    Yes. Your User ID may be your registered e-mail address. Hence, the embargo on the "@" symbol in User IDs (see above). This will prevent anyone else from using your e-mail address as his/her User ID. If anyone chooses to use an e-mail address as his/her User ID, it must match his/her registered e-mail address.

    I wanted to use "eBay" in my User ID. Why cant I?
    Only eBay employees may use "eBay" in their User IDs. This is to prevent anyone from appearing as though he/she were an employee of eBay.

    What if I am offended by someone elses User ID?
    Contact Customer Support at support@ebay.com. eBay does not allow obscenities, profanity or hateful language in User IDs. (See above)

    FINDING MY FRIENDS

    How do I find the e-mail address of someone with a User ID?
     Simply click on that persons User ID. Enter your User ID or registered e-mail address and your eBay password, and eBay will show you that persons registered e-mail address.

    What if I am a seller in a Dutch Auction?
    We will provide the e-mail addresses of your Dutch Auction winners to you in the End of Auction Notice. And we will also make it possible for you to access the e-mail addresses of Dutch Auction bidders online through the item pages. You will not be required to make multiple requests.

    What if I am a seller in several auctions and I need the e-mail addresses of several bidders?
     eBay will provide a page through which you will be able to request multiple e-mail addresses. This page will only require you to enter your User ID and your eBay password once.  You will not be required to make multiple requests.

    CHANGING MY LOOK...

    Can I change my User ID?
    Yes. When you change your User ID, eBay will give you a pair of "shades" to help you tell other eBay users about your new look. The shades icon will appear after your User ID for 30 days. During this time, your old User ID will be "embargoed." No one else will be able to use your old User ID until the 30-day period has expired.

    What does the "Shades" icon mean?
    The "shades" icon behind a User ID signifies that the user has changed his/her User ID within the last 30 days. The "shades' icon will appear after a User ID for 30 days following a successful change in User ID.

    What should I do if I see a "Shades" icon?
    Click on it! The "shades" icon behind a User ID means that person has changed his/her User ID within the last 30 days. Clicking on the "shades" icon will allow you to see that persons User ID history. This information will help you keep track of your friends and trading partners.

    If I change my User ID, how soon can I use the new User ID?
    Immediately. Your new User ID will be reflected in any auction in which you are participating. For example, suppose your User ID is "John," and you are the seller of a lamp. When you change your User ID to "Jane," the auction will then show "Jane" as the seller of the lamp even if the auction is currently running. Also, the "shades" icon will appear after "Jane" for 30 days following your successful change in User ID.

    How often can I change my User ID?
    A User ID may not be changed more than once in a 30-day period. This is to prevent confusion - mostly for the rest of us. There may be a very good reason for you to change your User ID more than once in 30 days. Please contact support@ebay.com for help and explain your situation to them.

    But we must caution you against frequently changing your User ID. Your User ID is a valuable asset to participating on eBay. You may find that your trading partners will come to recognize you by your User ID and associate certain characteristics with your User ID, much like they might with your Feedback Rating. Given this, you may want to choose a User ID that is suitable for a long, successful life on eBay.

    Choose a User ID that is suitable for a long, successful life on eBay. And choose wisely!

    How do I change my User ID?
    Simply go to the Change User ID page and follow the instructions.

    Can eBay change my User ID?
    eBay reserves the right to modify, alter or suspend any User ID at any time, at our sole discretion and without notice, for any reason whatsoever.

    Does eBay keep track of all my User ID changes?
    Yes. When you change your User ID, you create a User ID history which is accessible by any registered eBay user. You can see any user's User ID to show the history.

    A User ID history may look something like this:

    User ID: Norma Jean Baker
    Effective Date: Feburary 1, 1998
    End Date: Feburary 7, 1998

    User ID: Marilyn Monroe
    Effective Date: Feburary 7, 1998
    End Date: Present

    MAKING OTHER CHANGES...

    Does my Feedback Rating follow me when I change my User ID?
    Yes. Much like changing your registered e-mail address, changing your User ID will prompt eBay to transfer your Feedback Rating and other important account information to your new User ID.

    What if I want to change my registered e-mail address?
    If you change your registered e-mail address, you can still keep the same User ID. eBay allows you to use your registered e-mail address and your User ID interchangeably. This means that wherever you are asked for your registered e-mail address, you may provide your User ID  and vice versa.

    PROTECTING US FROM SHILL BIDDING

    Wont this make it easier for shill bidders to hide?
    No. If someone changes his/her User ID, it is reflected immediately in a current auction.

    For example, "Sylvia" is the seller in an auction and wants to change her User ID to "Cybill" to shill bid. Sylvia successfully changes her User ID to Cybill and goes to bid on her own item. No problem, right? Wrong. Sylvia gets to the Item Page and sees that Cybill is now listed as the seller. So, shill bidding as Cybill isnt any easier than shill bidding as Sylvia.

    Also, the "shades" icon will follow a new User ID for 30 days. This is to alert other eBay users of that persons new look. And, like a persons Feedback History, eBay has provided you an easy, accessible User ID History. You may view your own or any other eBay users User ID History at any time.

    How does this prevent someone from bidding on an item, changing his/her User ID, and bidding again?
    lets go back to Sylvia.

    "Sylvia" is the seller in an auction and wants to change her User ID to "Cybill" to shill bid. Sylvia successfully changes her User ID to Cybill and goes to bid on her own item. No problem, right? Wrong. Sylvia gets to the Item Page and sees that Cybill is now listed as the seller. So, shill bidding isnt any easier for Cybill than shill bidding is for Sylvia.

    What will eBay do to help protect me from someone changing his/her User ID to shill bid?
    eBay uses the "shades" icon to let you know that someone has changed his/her User ID. An auction can last three, five or seven days. But when you change your User ID, the "shades" icon will appear after your name for 30 days to alert other users of your new look.

    When you see a "shades" icon, click on it! Clicking on the shades icon will allow you to see that persons User ID history. This information will help you keep track of your friends and trading partners.
