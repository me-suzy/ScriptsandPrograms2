Finding Stuff: Step 2 of 3
Browsing the Listings

To get to the listings pages, click on "Listings" at the top or bottom of any eBay page, or click on the name of a category on the \$HOMENAME homepage. The listings at eBay are divided into categories and sub-categories. Let's take a look at a sample Listings Page for the category, "Computers : Hardware."

Listings Pages are composed of 4 main sections.

1. Categories.
Here we can specify sub-categories that we are interested in. Clicking on a sub-category focuses the listings so that only items that are related to a particular sub-category will be shown.

You can also restrict the listings to show only items that are New Today (posted in the last 24 hours), Ending Today (ending in the next 24 hours), Completed (already ended), or Going, Going, Gone (ending in the next 3 hours).

2. Featured Auctions.
These are auctions that have been featured by eBay sellers. It costs extra to have your auction appear in this section.

3. Hot Items.
Hot items are those that have already received more than 30 bids.

4. All Items.
The entire list of items in the specified category (Computers : Hardware in this example), is shown here. Often, there are several pages of listings. In this example, there are 69 pages, and we are currently on the first page. Clicking on an item title will show you the detailed description of that item, and allow you to place a bid on that item.

Some items have a icon next to their titles, meaning that the item description includes a picture of the item.

Items that have been posted in the last 24 hours are marked with a icon.

This sample Listings Page has been shortened for this tutorial.
