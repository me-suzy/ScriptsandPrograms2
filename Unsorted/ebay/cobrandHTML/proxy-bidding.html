Proxy Bidding
Using your \$HOMENAME Elf

So here's the deal: you've found a really cool item on eBay, you're willing to pay $25.00 for it, and right now the current bid price is only $2.25. What next?

Well, you could bid just $2.50 and probably be the high bidder... but what happens when you leave your computer and someone else comes in and bids $2.75? Do you have to sit in front of your computer day and night until the auction ends to make sure you win? Thanks to \$HOMENAME's automatic proxy bidding, the answer is no.

Here's how \$HOMENAME works (so that you don't have to!).

Let's take a look at a simulation of the auction:

That's how a live real-world auction goes, but instead of having everyone sit in front of their computers for days on end waiting for an auction to end, we do it a little differently. Everyone has a little magical elf (aka proxy) to bid for them and all you need to do is tell your elf the most you want to spend for that item and he'll sit there and outbid other elves for you, until his limit is reached. So it goes something like this:

You've had a fierce bidding war, and come out victorious, without even batting an eyelid or breaking a sweat! (you were probably out playing golf at the time). Furthermore, even though your maximum bid was $25.00, you win with a bid of only $8.50!

That's how eBay's little green bidding elves work! And even better than your best friend, they can keep a secret, so no one will know what your maximum bit amount was! How's that for a real pal? (No, eBay will not let you take one home to keep! They keep the Umpa-Loompa's, who run eBay, company when they aren't bidding for you!)

Happy treasure hunting!

Note: Proxy bidding is not used for Dutch auctions.
