Benchmarks

eBay was launched in September 1995. The first auction was conducted on Labor Day, and, within a few weeks, buyers and sellers began flocking to the service. Needless to say, Pierre, eBay's founder, was like a proud parent.

Today, we continue to take great pride in our business benchmarks.

  • The eBay community includes more than two million registered users.

  • eBay users buy and sell items in more than 1,000 categories. Users can find the unique and the interesting on eBay – everything from chintz china to chairs, teddy bears to trains, furniture to figurines.

  • Each day, eBay hosts over 1.8 million auctions, with over 250,000 new items joining the "for sale" list every 24 hours.

  • On a seven-day average, eBay receives approximately 800,000 bids per day. That computes to 33,000 bids per hour, 554 bids per minute and 3 bids per second.

  • More than 45 million auctions have been completed on eBay since its inception.

  • Each month, the site receives more than one billion page impressions.

  • In December 1998, eBay users averaged more than 126.7 minutes on the site – making eBay the second most popular web site by user minutes according to online research company MediaMetrix, the leading Web audiences measurement company.
