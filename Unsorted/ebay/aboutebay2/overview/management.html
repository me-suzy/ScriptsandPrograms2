Pierre Omidyar
Chairman and Founder

A casual dinner conversation in 1995 between Pierre and his fiancée changed the face of Internet commerce and began the development of one of the strongest online communities in the world.

His fiancée's passion for Pez™, the colorful candy dispensers, and lack of trading partners in the San Francisco Bay Area sparked Pierre to find a solution—an online auction, now called eBay, was the answer.

Pierre has a history of success with some of the most visionary companies in the Silicon Valley and has established a personal reputation among the Valley's tech elite.

A co-founder of Ink Development Corp. in 1991, Pierre was instrumental in leading the firm into the uncharted waters of Internet shopping in 1993. The company, then renamed eShop, was subsequently purchased by Microsoft in early 1996.

With an acute vision of the demand for consumer trading via the Internet, Pierre initially developed eBay as an experiment to assess demand for this type of trade. When all expectations were exceeded, he resigned from his full-time position in Developer Relations for General Magic to further develop the eBay service. While at General Magic, Pierre developed the first Web application for Magic Cap and contributed to other significant Internet projects.

Prior to co-founding Ink, Pierre was a successful developer for Claris, a subsidiary of Apple Computer, and for other Macintosh-oriented software development companies.

Pierre, a graduate of Tufts University (BS, Computer Science, 1988), is looking forward to marrying into a world-class Pez collection!

Meg Whitman
Chief Executive Officer

Meg's expertise in brand building combined with her consumer technology experience make her uniquely qualified to guide eBay, the most popular online trading community. The opportunity to strengthen an online community and brand that has changed so many lives for the better, convinced Meg that eBay was the right venue for her talents.

Prior to her current position, Meg was the General Manager of Hasbro Inc.'s Preschool Division. In this position, Meg was responsible for global management and marketing of two of the world's best-known children's brands, Playskool and Mr. Potato Head. During her tenure, Meg oversaw the reorganization of the Preschool Division and its resulting return to profitability.

From 1995 to 1997, Meg was President and CEO of Florists Transworld Delivery (FTD), the world's largest floral products company. While at FTD, Meg oversaw the transition from a florist-owned association to a for-profit, privately-owned company. In addition, she led the launch of FTD's Internet strategy, with a Web site (www.ftd.com) that includes individual florists' shops under the FTD banner.

Before FTD, Meg served as President of the Stride Rite Corporation's Stride Rite Division where she was responsible for the launch of the highly successful Munchkin baby shoe line and the repositioning of the Stride Rite brand and retail stores. She had also been Executive Vice President for the Keds Division and served as Corporate Vice President of Strategic Planning.

Meg spent 1989 to 1992 at the Walt Disney Company, highlighted by her work as the Senior Vice President of Marketing of the Disney Consumer Products Division. During this time, she managed key aspects of Disney's major businesses, set up vertically integrated Disney publishing operations, developed the strategy for Disney's entry into the book publishing business, and led the acquisition of Discover Magazine. She also worked for eight years at Bain & Co.'s San Francisco office where she was Vice President. Meg began her career at the Procter and Gamble Company in Cincinnati where she worked in brand management from 1979 to 1981.

Meg joined the eBay team as President and CEO in March of 1998. She is a graduate of the Harvard Business School (MBA, 1979) and of Princeton University (BA, Economics, 1977).

Gary Bengier
Chief Financial Officer

Gary brings more than 18 years of professional experience to eBay. His experience has shown particular emphasis on building administrative infrastructure for growing companies on business strategy, and on execution of complex financial transactions.

Prior to joining eBay, Gary was CFO for VXtreme, a Silicon Valley startup creating products which streamed video over the Internet. Before VXtreme, Gary was the top financial officer at Compass Design Automation, a provider of software for electronic circuit design. At Compass, he was a key contributor to strategy and business planning and led teams to build the company's worldwide administrative and financial systems and infrastructure.

Gary has also held senior financial positions at Kenetech Corp. and Qume Corp., where he participated in numerous debt and equity financing transactions. At Qume, these included efforts to take Qume subsidiaries public in foreign markets, and financial leadership of a management buyout.

Prior to joining Qume in 1989, Gary spent six years at Bio-Rad Laboratories and held various financial management roles as the company grew from $65 million to $200 million. Gary also spent several years as a management consultant for Touche Ross & Co.

As a child, Gary collected coins and fossils. Today, Gary is a collector of many experiences. In addition to being a pilot, he has climbed the Matterhorn in Switzerland, loves backpacking, and is currently making a classic Bordeaux blend from grapes grown in his backyard vineyard.

Gary is a graduate of the Harvard Business School (MBA, 1981) and of Kent State University (BBA, Computer Science and Operations Research, 1977).

Brian Swette
Senior Vice President of Marketing

With more than 20 years of experience in consumer brand marketing, Brian's leadership and expertise is making eBay a leading brand and the new generation online marketplace. Brian is overseeing International outreach, marketing, and customer support efforts for eBay. His goal is to make eBay the most fun, exciting, and accessible commerce site ever.

Prior to joining eBay, Brian was Executive Vice President and Chief Marketing Officer for the Pepsi-Cola Company. In this capacity, he was responsible for the worldwide marketing and advertising efforts for all of Pepsi-Cola's brands, encompassing established trademarks such as Pepsi, Diet Pepsi, Mountain Dew, 7UP, Mirinda, Slice, Lipton Iced Tea, Lipton Brisk, All Sport, Aquafina, and Starbucks Frappuccino coffee drink. He directed business strategy, brand architecture, and new product activity as well.

Under Brian, Pepsi introduced widely popular advertising campaigns, including Pepsi's well-known "generation" equities. Brian also led the resurgence of Mountain Dew to become the fastest growing beverage with $4 billion in sales.

In prior assignments for Pepsi-Cola North America, Brian served as Executive Vice President of Marketing, Senior Vice President & General Manager of New Beverage Ventures, Director of Operations, and Vice President of National Marketing. He joined Pepsi-Cola International in 1981, working initially as Country Manager Brazil, followed by International Marketing Planning and Development. Before joining Pepsi, Brian was Brand Manager at Procter & Gamble.

Besides his profound collection of Pepsico items, Brian also collects Monopoly games, depression glass and sports memorabilia. Someday, Brian hopes to find carved Santos figurines through eBay. Brian holds a B.A. degree in Economics from Arizona State University.

Steve Westly
VP of Marketing and Business Development

In his day-to-day operations, Steve leads many armies. One is the marketing and business development staff at eBay with the mission of cultivating eBay's brand. The other army is his collection of more than 2,000 Airfix hand-painted toy soldiers.

When Steve joined eBay in 1997 and discovered that these icons of his childhood were available via auction, he began his collection in earnest.

While at eBay, Steve has created and executed strategic partnership agreements with more than 60 of the largest companies in the Internet industry including: AOL, Yahoo!, Excite, Netscape, USA Today, Tripod, and many others.

Prior to his current post with eBay, Steve was the Vice President of WhoWhere?, the largest directory for finding people and businesses on the Internet. He has held a number of executive positions in Silicon Valley in the last 15 years.

Before developing his Internet expertise, Steve served five years on the faculty at Stanford's Graduate School of Business with an emphasis in political science. As a tribute to his teaching, three of his current eBay colleagues are his former Stanford students.

Prior to teaching at Stanford, Steve was the president of Codd and Date International, a 70-person relational database consulting firm with seven offices throughout the U.S. and Europe. Steve also served as an investment banker for Bridgemere Capital, a San Francisco-based investment banking firm.

Steve is the editor of two books on the utility industry and has extensive public policy experience. Steve has been listed in Who's Who of American Politics since 1983 and has held senior policy positions in local, state, and federal government.

Steve is a graduate of Stanford University (MBA, 1983; BA, History, 1978).

Michael Wilson
Vice President, Engineering

Michael's more than 20 years of experience in the online community and commerce fields has been instrumental in eBay's development of the next generation of the online marketplace.

Prior to joining eBay, Michael was the Vice President of Software Development for WELL Engaged, a spin-off from The WELL, specializing in community and communications products for the Web. WELL Engaged's first customers included such prestigious companies as The Wall Street Journal, Warner Music, Playboy, and Quote.com.

Michael's career began at Macy's California, where he was a key player in several retail MIS advancements including point of sale capabilities, online credit authorization, and electronic merchandise ticketing and tracking.

After Macy's, Michael oversaw several key departments and projects for Chevron, including the management and software support for Chevron's 10,000+ member online user community.

As an early employee at Oracle Corporation, Michael was a key developer of the company's mainframe products. He later became the Director of the Macintosh Division, after creating the first version of Oracle for the Macintosh.

At Ink Development Corp., Michael became the Chief Architect and AT&T; Project Manager for the electronic shopping division. This division eventually absorbed the entire company and became eShop. eShop was acquired by Microsoft in 1996.

Michael has also held key positions at daVinci Time and Space, where he worked on ITV-based community, and at Neuron Data, where he worked to bring artificial intelligence together with modern relational and object-oriented database technology.

Jeff Skoll
Vice President, Strategic Planning and Analysis

Prior to joining eBay, Jeff successfully managed the distribution channels for Knight-Ridder Information. Transforming third-party resellers into the most profitable line of business for the company, Jeff grew KRII resellers into a $15M channel.

Jeff previously founded two companies in Toronto, Canada. Skoll Engineering, a computer consulting firm created in 1987, catered to Fortune 500 and large government clients. The company was lead contractor in the development of a number of challenging and significant projects, including a nuclear coolant leakage detection system, a nationwide inventory and billing system, and an automated financial accounting system for a large corporate client. Micros on the Move Ltd., a computer rentals company, was co-founded by Jeff as an adjunct to Skoll Engineering in 1990. The company provided rental equipment to small business clients and quickly grew to assume a leading role in the Toronto market.

Jeff is a graduate of Stanford University (MBA, 1995) and the University of Toronto (BS, Electrical Engineering, 1987).
