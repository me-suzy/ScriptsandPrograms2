 Judy Williams

While stories abound of newbies who were suddenly empowered by the Web, Judy William's experience with eBay has provided some particularly positive and uplifting moments, allowing her to transition off welfare and increasingly on to self-sufficiency.

Born and reared in Massachusetts, Judy had every reason to believe she would be successful. She was schooled in nursing and certified as a registered nurse. After moving to Atlanta, Texas, however, she contracted lyme disease. The disease left Judy tired and forgetful. She was unsuited for nursing.

By the late 1980s, Judy was divorced, single mother struggling to raise her daughter alone. Unable to hold down a full-time job, she dabbled in tanning, buck-skinning and weaving - and traveled across the United States, trying to sell her goods at regional art shows.

In 1995, Judy acquired an antique shop. But selling antiques at the shop wasn't lucrative. By April 1997, Judy was a divorced, single mother on welfare - doing her best to keep her antique shop alive. When the welfare office advised Judy to go on disability, Judy said "I know there's something I can do."

A friend had told her about eBay.

Armed with a credit card, Judy bought a computer and got online. Soon, she was selling items on eBay and earning a living. She called the welfare office to tell them that she didn't need food stamps anymore. She is considering closing the antique shop.

"I turned down the disability," Judy said. "I wanted to make it."

Jim Watson

Jim Watson, retiree in Fort Myers, FL, has found in eBay a new outlet to trade collectibles and a new social network to enrich his life. He was searching for places to buy components to repair and upgrade his computer system when he came across eBay in November 1996 while surfing the Web. Upon discovering eBay's town bazaar atmosphere, his interest in eBay quickly shifted to one focused around his hobby of buying and selling collectibles. He took advantage of the access to the large pool of knowledgeable buyers.

Jim's connection to eBay further evolved when he discovered a new network of similarly gregarious collectors at the eBay Cafe, the site's gathering place. He regularly interacts with other traders there, swapping stories and learning new information about common interests. He has made new cyber-friends from around the world through eBay, people he would not have met if he hadn't gone online to shop for his computer parts. He visits many of these friends when they travel on vacation.

Time and again, there are events that reaffirm Jim's belief that eBay is a supportive and safe place to trade and make friends. In one instance, a woman in Berwyn, IL., was presented with a computer assembled from surplus parts donated by computer buffs on eBay so she could continue her interactions with traders on weeknights and weekends. A man in Canada found a book that Jim had long wanted and sent it as a gift; Jim sent a thank you with a special selection from his stamp collection. Jim also answered a request for help from a St. Louis man to set up a stamp club; Jim now serves as an advisor to the club.

Chris Campagna

Christine Campagna of Berwyn, IL, is on the road to self-improvement as a result of discovering eBay. She found eBay in the way many users found eBay - by accident. She was surfing the web in February 1996 and stumbled onto a list of online auctions.

Chris initially used eBay to buy and sell items, but later discovered the chat site. She developed relationships with other users, users who would later teach her hypertext markup language (HTML), the Web page design code.

Because Chris did not have a computer at home, her new cyber-friends missed her during weeknights and weekends when she couldn't go online. In an amazing show of outreach and support, more than 15 users secretly sent spare computer parts to another Illinois resident who assembled the parts together with her husband to form a functioning computer. Another user donated a printer. Since receiving the computer in May 1997, Chris has learned how to use spreadsheet, presentation and account software with the help of eBay users.
