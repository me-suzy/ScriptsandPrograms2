Helping People Trade

The History   eBay was launched by Pierre Omidyar as an experiment in electronic commerce. An early Internet enthusiast, Mr. Omidyar knew that people needed a central location to buy and sell unique items and to meet other users with similar interests. He started eBay to fulfill this need.

The Trust  The key to eBay's success is trust. Trust between the buyers and sellers who make up the eBay community. And trust between the user and eBay the company.

The Feedback Forum  eBay encourages active communication between buyers and sellers, who freely exchange information.

To dispel hesitancy sometimes associated with Internet commerce, eBay invented the industry's first electronic information exchange exclusive to one-to-one trading--the Feedback Forum.

Through eBay's innovative Feedback Forum, users can submit comments about their dealings with one another. These comments, both positive and negative, form online reputations that follow the user throughout the site. In this way, buyers and sellers quickly become familiar with one another - developing relationships, building trust and promoting an open and honest marketplace.

The Community  eBay is the home of a unique online community. Through the bulletin boards, users meet and get to know each other, discuss topics of mutual interest and petition one another for information. These bulletin boards are public forums that encourage open communication between users.

The community is also self-policing, and users frequently form "neighborhood watch" groups to help guard against misuse or violations of site etiquette.

eBay also encourages open and honest communication between the community and the company. Frequently, members of the community organize grassroots movements to improve the environment in which they work and play.
