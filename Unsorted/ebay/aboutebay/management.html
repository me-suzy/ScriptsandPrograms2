Pierre brings to eBay a history of success with some of the most visionary companies in Silicon Valley, and has established a personal reputation among the who's-who of the Valley.

A co-founder of Ink Development Corp. in 1991, Pierre was instrumental in leading the firm into the uncharted waters of Internet shopping in 1993. The company, then renamed eShop, was subsequently purchased by Microsoft in early 1996.

In 1995, Pierre accurately foresaw a demand for consumer trading over the Internet and developed the eBay service as an experiment to assess demand for this type of trade. With consumer usage having exceeded all expectations, Pierre resigned from his full-time position in Developer Relations for General Magic to further develop the eBay service. While at General Magic, Pierre developed the first Web application for Magic Cap, and contributed to other significant Internet projects.

Prior to co-founding Ink, Pierre was a successful developer for Claris, a subsidiary of Apple Computer, and for other Macintosh-oriented software development companies.

Pierre is a graduate of Tufts University (BS, Computer Science, 1988).


Assuming the mantle of President and CEO of eBay, Meg was previously the General Manager of Hasbro Inc.'s Preschool Division. In this position, Meg was responsible for global management and marketing of two of the world's best known children's brands, Playskool and Mr. Potato Head. The Preschool Division also develops and markets toys for the Barney and Arthur brands under license agreements. During her tenure, Meg oversaw the reorganization of the Preschool Division and its resulting return to profitability.

From 1995 to 1997, Meg was President and CEO of Florists Transworld Delivery (FTD), the world's largest floral products company. While at FTD, Meg oversaw the transition from a florist-owned association to a for-profit, privately-owned company. In addition, she led the launch of FTD's Internet strategy, with a Web site (www.ftd.com) that includes individual florists' shops under the FTD banner.

Before FTD, Meg served as President of the Stride Rite Corporation's Stride Rite Division where she was responsible for the launch of the highly successful Munchkin baby shoe line and the repositioning of the Stride Rite brand and retail stores. She had also been Executive Vice President for the Keds Division and served as Corporate Vice President of Strategic Planning.

Meg spent 1989 to 1992 at the Walt Disney Company, highlighted by her work as the Senior Vice President of Marketing of the Disney Consumer Products Division. During this time, she managed key aspects of Disney's major businesses, set up vertically integrated Disney publishing operations, developed the strategy for Disney's entry into the book publishing business, and led the acquisition of Discover Magazine.

She also worked for eight years at Bain & Co.'s San Francisco office where she was Vice President.

Meg began her career at the Procter and Gamble Company in Cincinnati where she worked in brand management from 1979 to 1981.

Meg is a graduate of the Harvard Business School (MBA, 1979) and of Princeton University (BA, Economics, 1977).


Gary brings more than 18 years of professional experience to eBay. His experience has shown particular emphasis on building administrative infrastructure for growing companies on business strategy, and on execution of complex financial transactions.

Prior to joining eBay, Gary was CFO for VXtreme, a Silicon Valley startup creating products which streamed video over the Internet. Before VXtreme, Gary was the top financial officer at Compass Design Automation, a provider of software for electronic circuit design. At Compass, he was a key contributor to strategy and business planning and led teams to build the company's worldwide administrative and financial systems and infrastructure.

Gary has also held senior financial positions at Kenetech Corp. and Qume Corp., where he participated in numerous debt and equity financing transactions. At Qume, these included efforts to take Qume subsidiaries public in foreign markets and financial leadership of a management buyout effort.

Prior to joining Qume in 1989, Gary spent six years at Bio-Rad Laboratories and held various financial management roles as the company grew from $65M to $200M. Gary also spent several years as a management consultant for Touche Ross & Co.

Gary is a graduate of the Harvard Business School (MBA, 1981) and of Kent State University (BBA, Computer Science and Operations Research, 1977).


With eBay, Steve has created and executed partner agreements with more than 30 of the largest companies in the Internet industry including: AOL, Yahoo, Excite, Netscape, USA Today, Tripod, and many others. Steve also manages the company's growing advertisting sales staff.

Steve was formerly the Vice President of WhoWhere?, the largest directory for finding people and businesses on the Internet. He has held a number of executive positions in Silicon Valley in the last 15 years. Before becoming active in the Internet area he served for five years on the faculty at Stanford's Graduate School of Business. Prior to teaching at Stanford, Steve was the president of Codd and Date International, a 70-person relational database consulting firm with seven offices throughout the U.S. and Europe. Steve also served as an investment banker for Bridgemere Capital, a San Francisco-based investment banking firm.

Steve is the editor of two books on the utility industry and has extensive public policy experience. Steve has been listed in the Who's Who of American Politics since 1983 and has held senior policy positions in local, state and federal government.

Steve is a graduate of Stanford University (MBA, 1983; AB, History, 1978).


Michael's more than 20 years of experience in the online community and commerce fields has been instrumental in eBay's development of the next generation of the online marketplace.

Prior to joining eBay, Michael was the Vice President of Software Development for WELL Engaged, a spin-off from The WELL, specializing in community and communications products for the Web. WELL Engaged's first customers included such prestigious companies as The Wall Street Journal, Warner Music, Playboy, and Quote.com.

Michael's career began at Macy's California, where he was a key player in several retail MIS advancements including point of sale capabilities, online credit authorization, and electronic merchandise ticketing and tracking.

After Macy's, Michael oversaw several key departments and projects for Chevron, including the management and software support for Chevron's 10,000+ member online user community.

As an early employee at Oracle Corporation, Michael was a key developer of the company's mainframe products. He later became the Director of the Macintosh Division, after creating the first version of Oracle for the Macintosh.

At Ink Development Corp., Michael became the Chief Architect and AT&T Project Manager for the electronic shopping division. This division eventually absorbed the entire company and became eShop. eShop was acquired by Microsoft in 1996.

Michael has also held key positions at daVinci Time and Space, where he worked on ITV-based community, and at Neuron Data, where he worked to bring artificial intelligence together with modern relational and object oriented database technology.
