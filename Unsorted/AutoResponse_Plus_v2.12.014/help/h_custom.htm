CUSTOM CONFIRMATION PAGES

When someone subscribes to one of your autoresponders via a web form, he/she will see a default confirmation page generated by the system. If the subscription fails, they will see a default failure page. The same applies when a contact clicks on the 'unsubscribe' link in one of your follow-up messages.

The default pages are very plain and contain no information about you or your company.

Instead of using the system generated success or failure pages, you can create your own. There are two types of custom pages:

  • Those which will be displayed to subscribers who have a particular tracking tag associated with their subscription.
  • Custom default pages that will be displayed to subscribers who have no tracking tag associated with their subscription.

Create your custom pages in your usual HTML editor and upload them to your web site. The custom default pages are set in the System Setup screen. Click Setup on the menu bar to go there. The fields you need to enter are:

  • Default custom subscribe success page URL
  • Default custom subscribe failure page URL
  • Default custom unsubscribe success page URL
  • Default custom unsubscribe failure page URL

To associate your custom pages with particular tracking tags, either create a new tracking tag or edit an existing one. The fields you need to enter are:

  • Custom subscribe success page URL
  • Custom subscribe failure page URL
  • Custom unsubscribe success page URL
  • Custom unsubscribe failure page URL

WHEN COULD THIS BE USED?

Imagine the scenario where you run an e-zine. You offer subscription on your home page and also on a popup page that appears whenever someone leaves your site. Both of these forms will subscribe your contact to one autoresponder. However, if you use a different tracking tag for each form, you can display different confirmation pages when someone subscribes - one for subscriptions taken from your home page and one for subscriptions tken from your popup.

You might have chosen to make these pages different because, for example, you want to offer a special bonus in one but not the other.
