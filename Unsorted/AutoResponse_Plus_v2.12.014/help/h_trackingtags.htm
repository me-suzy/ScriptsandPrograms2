TRACKING TAGS OVERVIEW

Tracking tags are used to track the source of subscriptions to your autoresponders. Subscriptions by all methods (e-mail, web forms and links) can contain tracking tags. By using tracking tags to analyze the source of your autoresponder subscriptions, you can, for example, target advertising more efficiently.

Tracking tags can also be used to control which custom subscribe and unsubscribe confirmation pages your subscribers will see.

To work with your tracking tags, click Tracking Tags on the menu bar. At any time and from any location, you can click Tracking Tags on the menu bar to go to your list of tracking tags.

The system's automatic code generator will automatically create the subscription code which you need to include in your web pages, e-mails, ezine ads etc. This code will contain the tracking tag you specify.

EXAMPLE USE OF TRACKING TAGS
You have created an autoresponder containing a free report on Internet marketing. Your aim is to build an opt-in e-mail list by inviting people to subscribe to your autoresponder.

You are intending to advertise your autoresponder in two Internet marketing related e-zines and on the home page of your web site. You want to track the success of your advertising by tracking which ad produces the most subscriptions to your autoresponder.

Create three tracking tags, one for your web site and one each for the two e-zines you will be advertising in.

When you generate the HTML code for your web-based subscription form, choose the tracking tag you set up for it.

When you create the ads for the e-zines, include the tracking tags like this...

"To get your free report, simply click the link below and hit send."
mailto:freebook@yourdomain.com?subject=TRA01

"To get your free report, simply click the link below and hit send."
mailto:freebook@yourdomain.com?subject=TRA02

When people subscribe to your autoresponder through the form on your web site or by clicking on a link in your ads, your campaign list will show where they came from. You can filter and export the campaign data by tracking tag, allowing you to analyze the results in, for example, a spreadsheet package.

There are three main tracking tag action:

  • Create a new tracking tag
  • Edit an existing tracking tags
  • Delete a tracking tag
  • Create a new tracking tag

This option allows you to create the tracking tags used to track the source of your autoresponder subscriptions. Tracking tags need to be created before using the automatic code generator so that they are available to choose.

  • Edit an existing tracking tag

For editing the properties of a previously created tracking tag.

  • Delete a tracking tag

For removing a tracking tag from your system.
